Proficiency Levels Guide

Each news article on Korean News English is assigned an estimated proficiency level to help learners choose articles that best match their current language skills. Below is a detailed rubric explaining the criteria for each level.

Proficiency Levels Rubric

Level Listening Speaking Reading Writing
Beginner Understands basic phrases and instructions. Can follow simple conversations on familiar topics. Uses basic sentences and common expressions. Can introduce themselves and engage in simple exchanges. Reads and understands short, simple texts. Recognizes common words and phrases. Writes simple sentences and phrases. Can fill out forms with basic personal information.
Lower Intermediate Understands main points in conversations on familiar topics. Can follow simple stories and instructions. Can engage in basic conversations on familiar topics. Uses more complex sentences but may struggle with fluency. Reads and understands longer texts on familiar subjects. Can grasp the main idea and some details. Writes paragraphs about familiar topics. Can describe experiences and events with some grammatical errors.
Upper Intermediate Understands most conversations on a variety of topics. Can follow detailed instructions and discussions. Communicates effectively on a wide range of topics. Uses complex sentences with fewer errors. Reads and understands complex texts. Can identify key details and infer meaning. Writes clear, detailed texts on various topics. Can argue a point of view and produce mostly error-free text.
Advanced Understands detailed instructions and complex conversations. Can follow discussions on various topics. Communicates fluently and effectively on a wide range of topics. Uses complex sentences with good accuracy. Reads and understands complex texts on a variety of subjects. Can infer meaning and identify key details. Writes clear and detailed texts on a variety of topics. Can argue a point of view and produce error-free text.
Proficient Understands virtually everything heard in various contexts. Can follow abstract and specialized discussions. Speaks fluently and spontaneously. Can express ideas precisely and persuasively in a structured manner. Reads and understands a wide range of demanding texts, including implicit meanings. Can critically evaluate content. Writes clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects. Can produce sophisticated and coherent writing.
